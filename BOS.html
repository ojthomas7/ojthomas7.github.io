This project formed the first half of my final year dissertation module. My report investigates the phenomenon of schlieren imaging, a method of visulaising fluctuations in the density of a transparent medium. In an extension to my lab work I explored background oriented schlieren, otherwise known simply as “BOS”. BOS is a modern schlieren technique that is not held to the same constraints as a classical schlieren system, and relies entirely on software. I developed my own BOS setup within Python with the objective of verifying the findings of the NASA airBOS investigation into the same subject, and to test our BOS software with our own footage. To the right is an example of my code in action, visualising the otherwise invisible shockwaves created by the super-sonic jets flying in formation.
